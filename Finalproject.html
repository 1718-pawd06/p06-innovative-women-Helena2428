Dr. Shirley Jackson Ph.D (1946-present)

"Follow your passion with persistence, magnified by intense preparation."

Dr. Shirley Jackson was born on August 5th 1946 in Washington D.C. She is an only child to parents George and Beatrice Jackson. Jackson's father George helped her with her interest in science by helping her with science projects.

In high school Jackson was in fast paced math and science classes and was class valedictorian in 1964. Jackson has earned both bachelor's and doctoral degrees. Jackson got her doctorate degree at MIT so she can encourage more African American women to attend the college she was attending.

In 1991 Jackson became a professor of physics where she taught undergraduate and graduate students She not only worked with telecommunications, she also spent her time volunteering at Boston City Hospital where she worked with children who were ill. Dr. Shirley Jackson began conducting research in 1976 until 1991 in Physics at the AT&T Bell Laboratories in Murray Mill where she focused on the layered materials of optical and electronic properties.

Dr. Shirley Jackson is most commonly known for her invention of Caller ID and Call waiting on our phones. Jackson was inducted into the hall of fame in 1998 for her inventions and has been given the National Medal of Science for Physical Science which was presented by Barack Obama in 2015. Jackson is the first African-American woman to earn a Ph.D in physics. She has also received a bachelor's and doctoral degree as well in the field of Physics. On July 1st 1999 Jackson became the 18th president of the RPI (Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute). She served as president of the Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute until August of 2017.
